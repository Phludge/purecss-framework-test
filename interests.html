Interests

Things I enjoy and brief history

TECH·NOIR


Growing up in the 80's, I was always a creative kid. Daydreaming, drawing, playing make-believe with detailed storylines, building little cities in the backyard sandpit, climbing trees, or building with LEGO indoors. A person with an active and inventive imagination is hardly ever bored. At school my favorite classes were Art, Science, and any time where I was able to use a computer. Those computers were usually an IBM PC/AT (same one I played Oregon Trail on) or some IBM PC clone, and a Macintosh Classic II for Computer Graphics class by the time I reached junior high. My first time connecting to the internet was in 1989. It was a Unisys ICON II at a neighbor's house. The ISP was CompuServe and it was my first time seeing a BBS (Bulletin Board System) being dialed into and hearing "modem breath." I was hooked.


This also led to a fascination with the telephone system. I learned about the vertical service codes, like *69 (last call return) or *67 (block caller ID), along with other codes, which everyone knew. Useful, but the codes I was more interested in were the ones used by telephone line installers and repair technicians for testing. My favorite was making the telephone ring-back and play a tone upon answering using *991 and the last 4 digits of your number. I'd hang up and have a few seconds to walk away and play innocent before it rang. I confused a lot of friends and family with that one as a youngster.


My first regular access to the internet was on my aunt's black Compaq Presario in the mid 1990s. Some years later, I got my own computer, which was a Gateway PC that I ordered from the Gateway store, built to my custom specifications. Seeing The World Wide Web take shape in the 90's was a fun time. Browsing web rings, file-sharing, chatting on IRC and AOL, which we briefly had as our ISP before getting EarthLink. EarthLink came with a free webcam when you signed up. This was around the time I was learning how to make a website, and was proud when I got that webcam connected to a web page where it would automatically refresh. Along with making web pages, I enjoyed doing some graphic design, and ASCII or ANSI art which was inspired by visiting various Bulletin Boards. Which led to me doing some art for a warez group or two. I also discovered my love for fractal art using programs like Ultra Fractal, TieraZon, and Apophysis. Between this time and the early 2000s I was upgrading and building PCs.


The 2000's are when I entered the work force. I worked at Mellon Marine scraping barnacles off of boats and painting them, as well as detailing the interior. The pay was decent, but it wasn't a tech job. Later on I got a job as a Phone Technician refurbishing Toshiba phones. I'd replace parts, erase the old EEPROMs for reprogramming, and sand down the plastic trim and bezzles to get a fresh coat of paint. I then went on to doing IT work after getting my foot in the door through a technical college. When I was a Jr Systems Analyst I worked on an HP-9000 Superdome running HP-UX. I wrote and ran shell scripts for it, ran tape backups, and ran operator jobs that handled financial data and spit out reports that had to be organized and delivered to federal credit union staff members in different departments. I've held many titles and wore many hats over the years. Support Technician, Technical Specialist, DevOps, Technical Writer, Sysadmin, and Web Developer.


I love books, gardening, hikes in the woods, and firepits. I also love space, philosophy, mythology, and exploring the nature of conciousness.
