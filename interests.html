Interests

Things I enjoy and brief history

TECH·NOIR


"This is where I can put a funny. witty, highly intelligent quote about something people think is cool."

Growing up in the 80's, I was always a creative kid. Daydreaming, drawing, playing make-believe with detailed storylines, building little cities in the backyard sandpit, or building with LEGO indoors. A person with an active and inventive imagination is hardly ever bored. At school my favorite classes were Art, Science, and any time where I was able to use a computer. Those computers were usually an IBM PC/AT (same one I played Oregon Trail on) or some IBM PC clone, and a Macintosh Classic II for Computer Graphics class by the time I reached junior high. My first time connecting to the internet was in 1989. It was a Unisys ICON II at a neighbor's house. The ISP was CompuServe and it was my first time seeing a BBS being dialed into.


Seeing The World Wide Web take shape in the 90's was a fun time. Browsing web rings, file-sharing, chatting on IRC and AOL, which we briefly had as our ISP before getting EarthLink. It came with a free webcam you when signed up. This was around the time I was learning how to make a website, and was proud when I got that webcam connected to a web page where it would automatically refresh.


I love nature, gardening, hikes in the woods, and firepits. I also love space and philosophy.
